# **Distinction**

## Definition

A **distinction** is an **ontic, persistent partition of a systemâ€™s state space**, produced by irreversible dynamics, such that the systemâ€™s future evolution depends asymmetrically on which side of the partition it occupies.

---

## Ontic core (what exists physically)

Ontically, a distinction exists when:

- a continuous degree of freedom becomes separated into **stable or metastable regions** of state space,
- transitions across those regions are **suppressed relative to thermal or stochastic noise**,
- the separation persists over a timescale long compared to the systemâ€™s characteristic fluctuation time,
- persistence is maintained through **dissipation and loss of microscopic reversibility**.

Such a partition exists independently of observation, interpretation, or representation.

---

## Relation to perspective (explicitly non-constitutive)

A distinction **may be accessed epistemically** by a system with a [[Perspective\|Perspective]], but perspective does not create the distinction.

Perspective determines:
- how an existing distinction is detected or resolved,
- how it is encoded (e.g. as a bit, category, or symbol),
- how it is used for inference, control, or action.

Ontic persistence constrains epistemic access; epistemic access does not generate ontic persistence.
