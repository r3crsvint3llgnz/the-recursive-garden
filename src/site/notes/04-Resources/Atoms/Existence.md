# Existence

**Existence** is the status of a physical pattern that **persists under interaction** and **exerts counterfactual causal influence** on other systems over time.

A system exists only insofar as it instantiates [[04-Resources/Atoms/Persistent Distinction\|persistent distinctions]] that survive [[04-Resources/Atoms/Noise\|noise]] and participate in ongoing [[Dynamics\|dynamics]] through constrained interaction. Momentary configurations or isolated patterns do not qualify, even if they are formally definable.

Existence is scale-relative and non-binary. Whether a pattern exists depends on the timescales, constraints, and couplings under which it continues to make a difference to other systems.

Through this role, existence:
- filters ontology to include only causally active, persistent structures,
- grounds entities in interaction rather than description,
- ties ‚Äúbeing‚Äù directly to physical consequences.

---
### Distillation

To exist is to continue making a difference.

---
### Why it matters

- **Distinction:** Only persistent distinctions qualify as entities.
- **Persistence:** Existence depends on resistance to noise-driven decay.
- **Time:** Existence requires duration and recordable interaction.
- **RDD coherence:** Prevents treating fleeting fluctuations or abstractions as ontological commitments.

### Conflicts with

- Purely logical or observer-dependent notions of existence.
- Views that treat existence as instantaneous or static.
- Ontologies that admit entities without causal consequences.
