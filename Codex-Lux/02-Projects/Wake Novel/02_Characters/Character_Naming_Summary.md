## Naming Conventions in 2250s Spacefaring Society

### Cultural Evolution
By the mid-2200s, humanity has undergone significant cultural blending due to:
- **150+ years of post-scarcity society** reducing ethnic conflicts over resources
- **80+ years of interstellar expansion** creating new regional identities
- **Multicultural space habitats** fostering cultural exchange and intermarriage
- **Solar system colonization** creating new forms of regional identity

### Surname Trends
- **Celestial Body Names**: People adopt surnames tied to their home regions
  - Examples: Vesta, Callisto, Titan, Europa, Ganymede, Ceres, Luna, Mars
- **Regional Identity**: Solar system location becomes more important than Earth ethnicity
- **Family Heritage**: Names may be passed down or newly adopted based on birthplace

### First Name Trends
- **Multicultural Blending**: Names reflect mixed heritage combining elements from:
  - Hispanic/Latino traditions
  - Chinese and East Asian cultures
  - Indian and South Asian heritage
  - African diaspora influences
  - European backgrounds
- **Futuristic Evolution**: Some names sound distinctive to the time period
- **Meaning-Based**: Names often chosen for symbolic significance (exploration, resilience, celestial themes)

### Examples of Naming Patterns
- **Amara Chen**: African/Latin + Chinese
- **Leila Titan**: Arabic + Saturn's moon
- **Sora Callisto**: Japanese + Jupiter's moon
- **Zara Europa**: Arabic + Jupiter's moon
- **Kai Ganymede**: Hawaiian/Asian + Jupiter's moon

## Usage Guidelines for Additional Characters

### For Main Characters
- Choose names that reflect multicultural heritage
- Consider celestial body surnames for space-born characters
- Ensure names fit the character's background and role
- Avoid stereotypical or overly exotic combinations

### For Supporting Characters
- Maintain consistency with established naming patterns
- Vary cultural combinations to show diversity
- Use celestial surnames to indicate origin/identity
- Consider symbolic meanings that support character functions

### For Background Characters
- Quick reference combinations from established patterns
- Regional consistency (characters from same area might share surname elements)
- Professional/role-appropriate choices
- Easy to pronounce and remember for readers
